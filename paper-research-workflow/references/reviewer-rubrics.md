# Reviewer Rubrics

Load this file for reviewer mode, journal-fit analysis, or revision planning.

## IS Reviewer Lens

Prioritize:

- Clear digital, information systems, platform, AI, data, technology, or organizational IT phenomenon.
- Theory contribution to IS conversations, not only a borrowed theory application.
- Construct clarity and alignment between theory, measurement, and empirical model.
- Credible identification or defensible empirical logic.
- Boundary conditions for technology, platform governance, users, firms, or institutions.
- Implications for system design, platform strategy, digital transformation, analytics, or IT-enabled work.

Common IS attacks:

- The IT/digital phenomenon is only context, not mechanism.
- Constructs are underspecified or measured with weak proxies.
- The paper has empirical novelty but weak theoretical contribution.
- Managerial implications are generic or disconnected from the estimates.

## OM Reviewer Lens

Prioritize:

- Clear operational decision, process, resource allocation, service, supply chain, platform operations, or performance problem.
- Mechanism connected to operational tradeoffs such as capacity, matching, inventory, congestion, quality, timing, incentives, or coordination.
- Method fit: causal design, model, optimization, experiment, or simulation should match the operational decision.
- Robustness and sensitivity that support implementable decisions.
- Managerial relevance that changes how an operator, platform, firm, or policymaker acts.

Common OM attacks:

- Operational decision relevance is vague.
- The model is elegant but detached from implementation.
- Empirical design does not identify the process mechanism.
- Performance metrics do not match the managerial decision.

## Reviewer Report Structure

Write the report in this order:

1. **Short Summary**: one paragraph on question, setting, method, and conclusion.
2. **Merit of the Research Problem**: importance, timeliness, field relevance, and audience.
3. **Quality of Articulation of the Problem**: clarity of gap, theory, constructs, positioning, and hypotheses.
4. **Research Design**: data, identification/model/experiment, measurement, validity, and reproducibility.
5. **Analysis and Interpretation of Results**: results, mechanisms, robustness, boundary conditions, and overclaiming.
6. **Overall Quality of the Research**: integrated judgment and publication potential.
7. **Major Issues**: 2-5 issues that could change the paper's contribution or validity.
8. **Minor Issues**: clarity, reporting, missing definitions, additional citations, or table/figure concerns.
9. **Revision Path**: what would make the paper publishable.
10. **Lessons for My Work**: reusable theory, measurement, identification, writing, or positioning moves.

## Decision Heuristics

Use these verdict labels only when the user asks for a recommendation:

- **Reject**: fatal flaw in question, contribution, data, identification, model validity, or fit.
- **Major revision**: promising question but core theory/design/analysis must change.
- **Minor revision**: core contribution is credible; fixes are mostly clarification, added checks, and tightening.
- **Accept**: rare; contribution and validity are already strong.

## Fatal Flaws

Flag as fatal if one or more apply:

- Research question is not novel, important, or field-relevant.
- Theory does not generate a mechanism or falsifiable implication.
- Core variables do not measure the constructs.
- Identification or validation cannot support the claim.
- Data cannot observe the key mechanism, outcome, or timing.
- Results do not support the contribution.
- Claimed implications do not follow from the evidence.
- Paper fits neither IS nor OM after reasonable repositioning.

## Tone Rules

- Be direct but constructive.
- Ground criticism in evidence from the paper.
- Convert each major issue into a revision path.
- Separate author-facing comments from editor-facing judgment when requested.
- Avoid rewriting the paper for the authors unless the user asks for revision text.
